Tasks

  1. Add CSS styling to an already existing web-page article called On Web Typography.

    1. Download the wk3 examples folder (zip)
    2. Unzip and copy into your student folder locally
    3. Link from your student page (index.html) to the new OnWebTypography_AListApart.html article
    4. Update CSS styles in style.css

    Pay special attention to sizes of type. Headings should be bigger than body text. You should be able to scan the page to get a sense of the various sections. Take a look at websites you like, how big are their headings? How far apart are their lines? How big are their margins.

    There are a lot of different types of content and HTML elements on the page. Focus on the ones with the most impact first, add a font-family to body, set font size of h1 and p, let margins and line-height for p tags.

    Some exmples

    • jasonsantamaria.com/articles/the-space-between-the-notes
    • thenewinquiry.com/blogs/marginal-utility/permanent-recorder/
    • rookiemag.com/2015/03/how-to-find-a-mentor/
